Five Signals to Watch
Open Window
Nirbhaya Fund civil society partnership: position before the formal call
What moved

The Parliamentary Standing Committee's 377th Report (March 25, 2026) confirmed that Rs 1,631 crore of the Nirbhaya Fund's lifetime allocation remains unreleased, and named the specific bottleneck for the first time: non-compliance with SNA SPARSH documentation requirements. A dedicated Compliance Support Cell has been recommended. MoWCD has signalled interest in civil society implementation partners but has not yet issued a formal call.

What to do

Organisations doing GBV prevention, survivor support, or women's safety infrastructure should prepare a one-page government partnership brief now, before a formal process opens a crowded field.

Source: Parliamentary Standing Committee on WCD, 377th Report, March 25, 2026
Open Window
NSFI 2025-30: gender-sensitive inclusion named for the first time
What moved

India's National Strategy for Financial Inclusion 2025-30, released in early 2026, explicitly names gender-sensitive financial inclusion as a priority for the first time in a major RBI-anchored policy document. The strategy calls for differentiated credit models for women, targeted digital literacy, and resilience-building measures. This is the policy alignment argument that organisations working on women's financial inclusion have been waiting for.

What to do

If your programme works on women's financial inclusion, digital access, or credit, cite the NSFI 2025-30 directly in your next donor proposal. The window to use this before the sector catches up is now.

Source: National Strategy for Financial Inclusion 2025-30, RBI, 2026
Watch This
SHE Marts: state NRLM consultations happening before guidelines drop
What moved

Budget 2026-27 announced SHE Marts as a significant SHG graduation mechanism, positioning federation women as retail operators. As of April 2026, financing mechanisms remain undefined, income protection is absent from implementation guidelines, and risk is concentrated back on SHG women. State NRLM teams in Rajasthan and UP have begun informal consultations with SHG federations ahead of formal rollout, which means the design is still live and contestable.

What to do

If your programme works with SHG federations, verify whether your state NRLM counterpart has received informal SHE Mart participation guidance. Document any informal guidance received: this is valuable evidence for the formal consultation process.

Source: Union Budget 2026-27; NRLM civil society monitoring, March 2026
Watch This
MGNREGS correction: most organisations still citing the wrong figure
What moved

The 2025-26 Gender Budget Statement corrected women's MGNREGS allocation from 33.6% to 46.5%. This is a significant correction, one that changes the adequacy argument for rural women's employment. Three months after the correction, most organisations in the sector are still citing 33.6% in donor reports and advocacy documents. Using the old figure in any document produced after March 2026 is a data error that technical reviewers will flag.

What to do

Check every active donor report, proposal, and advocacy document produced in the last 90 days. Replace 33.6% with 46.5% wherever MGNREGS gender allocations are cited. This is a one-hour audit that protects your organisation's credibility.

Source: Gender Budget Statement 2025-26, Ministry of Finance
Closing Soon
UN Women India CSO grants: Rajasthan and UP focus, deadline April 30
What moved

UN Women India has opened a CSO grants round for gender-responsive livelihoods research with Rajasthan and UP as priority states. The focus areas, women's economic empowerment and unpaid care work, align directly with the Economic Survey 2025-26's first-ever acknowledgment of unpaid care as a structural barrier. Organisations with existing field presence in these states and evidence-based programme documentation are well positioned.

What to do

Deadline is April 30, 2026. If your organisation works on women's economic empowerment or care economy in Rajasthan or UP, review the call criteria and align your proposal to the Economic Survey framing: it's now official government language.

Source: UN Women India, April 2026. Deadline: April 30, 2026.

First in South Asia
Regional Firsts
Policies that changed the playbook
South Asia has produced some of the world's most quietly radical gender policy innovations. These are the ones worth knowing, not just as history, but as arguments.
🇧🇩
Bangladesh
First country in the Global South to achieve gender parity at head-of-state level
Bangladesh ranked 24th globally in the 2025 WEF Gender Gap Index, the highest in South Asia for the ninth consecutive year. Driven by sustained female leadership in governance and high workforce participation rates, it outpaced economies with significantly higher per capita income.
WEF Global Gender Gap Index 2025
🇧🇹
Bhutan
First nation to make Gross National Happiness the constitutional measure of development
Bhutan's 2008 constitution embedded Gross National Happiness as the guiding principle of state policy, a direct challenge to GDP as the primary measure of progress. The framework explicitly includes gender equity, cultural preservation, and psychological wellbeing as development indicators that economic metrics miss.
Constitution of Bhutan, 2008
🇮🇳
Kerala, India
World's largest women-led community enterprise network: Kudumbashree
Launched in 1998, Kudumbashree became the world's largest women-run poverty eradication programme, now covering over 4.5 million women in self-help groups across Kerala. Its offline-first, community-trust model is the reference point for why digital financial inclusion requires community infrastructure, not just connectivity.
Launched 1998, still operational

UN Population Fund, India
Strengthening Multi-Sectoral GBV Response Systems: Model OSCs and Shelter Homes
Closes April 17 MP, Odisha, Rajasthan, Karnataka, Punjab, AP 8-month TA package
Deadline
17 Apr
5 days left
What they're funding
An 8-month technical assistance package for NGOs or academic institutions to strengthen GBV response at 12 sites across six states. Focus areas: survivor-centered case management, infrastructure audits of One Stop Centres (OSCs) and Shakti Sadan shelter homes, staff mentoring, economic empowerment pathways for shelter residents, and district-level service mapping. Organisations must demonstrate proven GBV case management experience and multi-sectoral coordination capacity.
Southward Insight
This call is precisely at the intersection of OSC design drift and Nirbhaya Fund utilisation — two signals we've been tracking since March. UNFPA's language on "survivor-centered care" and "multi-sectoral coordination" maps directly onto the Parliamentary Standing Committee's March 25 recommendations. Organisations applying should cite both the 377th Committee Report and the UNFPA Essential Services Package framework in their methodology sections. Karnataka is explicitly included — directly relevant for GBV organisations in that state.
Language to use: "Multi-sectoral coordination," "survivor-centered case management," "disability-inclusive care," "economic empowerment pathways," "model OSC." Align to UNFPA's Essential Services Package for Women and Girls Subject to Violence. Cite state-level NHM and MoWCD partnership experience if available.

SBI Foundation
CSR — State Bank of India
SBIF Sashakti: Women's Empowerment and Non-Traditional Livelihoods
Closes April 20 Pan-India Rs 1–4 crore, 1–3 years
Deadline
20 Apr
8 days left
What they're funding
CSR grants of Rs 1 crore to Rs 4 crore for 1–3 year projects focused on women's economic empowerment in non-traditional livelihoods, including women in transport, and support for women involved in commercial sex work. Emphasis on SDGs 3, 5, 8, 10, and 17. Phased funding based on performance. Eligible applicants: Indian registered non-profits. FCRA not mandatory for CSR funding.
Southward Insight
SBI Foundation's Sashakti programme is one of the few CSR windows explicitly including women in non-traditional and informal livelihoods — categories that USAID previously funded and that Indian foundations have been reluctant to touch. The transport sector focus aligns with GIZ India's ongoing gender-inclusive mobility work, which could provide a policy hook. Proposals that link to NSFI 2025-30 language on women's financial inclusion will read as policy-current to the review committee.
Language to use: "Non-traditional livelihoods," "economic independence," "SDG 5 and 8 alignment," "sustainable livelihood ecosystem," "phased outcome-based implementation." Cite NSFI 2025-30 for financial inclusion framing. Reference Economic Survey 2025-26 unpaid care acknowledgment for care economy programmes.

What they're funding
UNFPA India Country Office is inviting proposals from NGOs and academic institutions for two linked research projects: (1) Reimagining the Elder Care Economy in India, and (2) Gender and Climate Change. Both must generate actionable, evidence-based recommendations for policy and practice. Organisations may apply for one or both. Contingent on successful agreement with the partner.

The elder care economy brief connects directly to the Economic Survey 2025-26's unpaid care work acknowledgment — the first time a major government document named this as a structural barrier. Research proposals that anchor to this acknowledgment are immediately policy-relevant. The gender-climate intersection also maps to GIZ India's GSDP green skills work and the NSFI 2025-30 resilience framework. Organisations with existing care economy or climate-gender data are best positioned.
Language to use: "Care economy," "unpaid care work," "gender-climate nexus," "actionable policy recommendations," "evidence-based practice." Anchor to Economic Survey 2025-26 Section on unpaid care (Chapter on Gender). For climate brief, cite NSFI 2025-30 resilience framing and NFHS-5 data.
